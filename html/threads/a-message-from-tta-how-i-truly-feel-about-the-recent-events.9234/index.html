I know that many of us have felt the pain of Tuesdays attack, and faced it with worry and uncertainty.


I for one am deeply sadden this had to happen, my deepest sympathies go out to the families that were lost. I share in your pain, I know the horror and despair of losing loved ones to another's control, and being unable to do anything.


It's a dark Hell, we as Americans are now facing, and it is in this time we need to be tolerant of others, and not lose sight that we are a civilized society.


Yes, I especially want justice served, but let's not take it out on the innocent Americans who had nothing to do with it. We are human beings, let us behave like human beings, and not resort to these cowards level.


These people who did this crime, are not human, they have no respect for human life, they take life with no remorse, and we need to get this Evil eradicated in the most expedient fashion.


However, in the mist of all that's happen, I also ask that people not take this as an opportunity to begin with the John Titor's prophecies or other claims. Besides all the continuing bomb threats, and of things yet unclear as to who done it, let's not add to this type of domino-effect hysteria.


I hate to see Dooms-Day cults pop up on the internet and in neighborhoods, and religious radicals preaching at the side of the roads, that the end of the world is coming. This is neither the time nor the place to be doing this. I hope that we as a people understand that this is a time to be appropriate, and address issues with the facts, not with assumptions.


Let's keep what's left of our sanity, and heal before we panic our selves to a frenzy. I know I can't tell everyone out there this, but please keep what I've said in the back of your mind somewhere. Maybe you can console a troubled co-worker or friend who feels anger and wants to take it out on someone and do something foolish.


I know I wrote quite a bit on this, and if your still reading it this far, thanks for reading . God-bless us all.


Sincerely yours,


Javier Cortez S.
