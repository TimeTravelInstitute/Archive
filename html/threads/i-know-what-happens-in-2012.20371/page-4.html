Wow, the happenings of 2012 have been known for thousands of years....at any rate, time travel is impossible.
Click to expand...
Though his statement really isn't a good physical theory it is an excellent example of a falsifiable statement.

"Time travel is impossible" is a good statement of a falsifiable proposition. If you find just one verifiable instance where any form of time travel is accomplished you have falsified his theory. You are then faced with the proposition of adjusting your theory such that it either generally allows time travel or limits time travel to the exception that was found.

Frankly, the latter is the better proposition. If you adjust the theory to generally allow time travel you are back to an unfalsifiable proposition. If you suggest that time travel is generally allowable and if you never find another instance where time travel is allowed you can never falsify the theory - even if you try forever to find another exception and fail to discover it.

Einstein's theory of special relativity falsified Newtonian relativity but kept it as a valid interpretation of mechanics within the limit of its domain - instances where the velocities involved are much less than the speed of light.
