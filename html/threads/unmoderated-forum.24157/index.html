Exactly. This would be a subsection for people with more extreme viewpoints. I want to hear those opinions, but also want to keep the site friendly for newcomers (particularly the politics, current events and religion forums). Anyone using it to simply bitch about someone here would get their thread removed - Stuff like that is better left to private messages so things can get worked out.

From Wikipedia:
Lunatic fringe is a term used to characterize members of a political or social movement as extremists with eccentric or fanatical views. The term was popularized by Theodore Roosevelt, who wrote in 1913 that, "Every reform movement has a lunatic fringe."

With all the insanity 2016 has brought out in people, 2017 is going to be even more interesting. Giving voice to those viewpoints while still allowing us to stay on topic elsewhere would be interesting to see.

I do see where the one person who responded "no" would be coming from though (I can't see who voted what, by the way). It'd be carefully looked at, just not interfered with unless someone broke one of those cardinal rules listed above. If it ends up being that most people are against it, we won't pursue it as we're looking make the place interesting, not drive anyone away.

There's definitely that chance, but only if a) we don't do our jobs and b) nobody here helps us out by reporting things that break the cardinal rules of that forum.

While this forum would be exempt from the usual moderator oversight, the rules from my first post would still apply. Members would be able to opt-out (or opt-in) to this forum; what the default is depends on whether I want anything there to get indexed by search engines. Haven't thought that far ahead yet, but this would be a setting in your profile that adds/removes you from a usergroup who is able to see this forum in the first place.

This is NOT a forum for trolling. Stuff like that would be easy to spot a mile away and is easily dealt with, but I do see your point and that's something to think about. Could be that we don't do this until we're more active, but it's an interesting idea.
