Timely1, I would be happy to offer some level-headed insight, for whatever it's worth. It may be a bit of a shot in the dark, considering the lack of details provided regarding the incident, but perhaps it will be helpful anyway.

Concerns of pedophiles and kidnappers aside, it would be hard to disagree that there is something quite unnerving about the kind of event you described. I am giving you the benefit of the doubt that you are telling the truth and reporting the incident as you saw it.

I'll say right off the bat that I can relate. Over the course of my life, I have experienced a series of dreams, waking life events, and apparently meaningful synchronicities that can only be called eerie. It's the sort of situation to which most reasonable people will say "there's no way to prove any of it as 'paranormal', but darn it, it sure seems like there's something to it." Being an empiricist at heart, and wanting to maintain my sanity, I have had to develop a kind of open-minded rationalism in order to feel that I am doing all I can to understand my strange experiences as objectively as possible.

During my teenage years, before I was able to develop this pseudo-detatchment, I found myself sabotaged by my own credulity more times than I care to remember. Let me warn you, a blind search undertaken with no solid evidence in mind is likely to lead you in circles. I urge you to follow this lead, as I'm sure you will anyway. Mysteries like this are, in the minds of many, the core of human experience. But when you do go about searching, I strongly recommend that you not come to any premature conclusions about the issue; until you know more, it might be as dangerous to discredit your intuitions as it would be to lose yourself in them. Approach this as a scientist or a journalist would. I know how difficult it is to examine objectively a situation in which you are a subjective observer, but the rewards can be well worth it.

I know you probably know all this already, but living in the beating heart of the New Age movement, I have seen too many people driven half mad by chasing false leads... myself included.
