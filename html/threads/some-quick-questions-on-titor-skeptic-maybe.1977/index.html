SadOne,

<<1) Would, by default of him being from the future, especially with all said radiation, have advanced strains of bacteria, so wouldn't he have basically killed a vast amount of anyone around him?>>

We've discussed the problem of alien bacteria and virii. If he's from the future, 36 years from now, he would be bring along strains of bacteria and virii that would have naturally mutated without the radiation. So, yes, he would be a possible vector for new diseases against which we would have no defense.

<<2) He refers to the Y2K problem, or at least as most think, and says it like Pearl Harbor never getting attacked, so wouldn't that mean that in his timeline something did happen and therefore affect his timeline, not ours?>>

That's probably one of those questions that can't be answered. He didn't give sufficient information to make any judgments about it. Y2K wasn't a problem and was never going to be a problem in the sense that Gary North said that it would be. It shouldn't be a problem for him either.

<<3) Why would he have to come back here for the IBM? They have the technology to make a time machine but not a lousy old computer?>>

It was a silly premise for justifying a time trip, wasn't it. Add to it a private vacation and it becomes really weird.

There's a bit of a mismatch in abilities there. They have Albert Einstein running the physics lab and Fred Flintstone running the software development lab.

<<4) The pictures he took of his time machine, all so low res. ... the only thing I know that could be that bad on the computer would be a Kodak picture disk, and if said technology is that much better in the future, then why would he be using a picture disk? He was quite coy about revealing any email addresses. And he used Hotmail-type email. If he was being truthful when he said that he had had "numerous online discussions" there should be people other than Pamela who participated in them. So far they have not appeared.
