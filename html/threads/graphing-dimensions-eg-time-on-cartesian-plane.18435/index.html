Re: Graphing Dimensions (eg. time) on Cartesian Pl

Nobb:

Well, since we have only three dimensions of space to work with in visualizing larger numbers of dimensions, we are somewhat limited in terms of our illustrative power. Basically, when working with visual representations drawn on paper, we can not draw a graph that makes use more than three mutually perpendicular axes. When dealing with pure math, we can conceive of arbitrarily numerous dimensions, as the maths used in physics are equipped to deal with such things, but graphic representations are not.

I've found that the best way to visually represent time, hyperspace, or more obscure dimensions such as one that represents a spectrum of quantum wavefunctions, is to draw out an X Y Z matrix and eliminate one dimension of space. That is, use a 2space as a stand-in for a 3space and use the third axis to represent the extraspatial dimension. It's sort of a cop-out, but it's about the best we can do short of wracking our brains on theoretical math-spaces.
