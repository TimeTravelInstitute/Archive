henry,

There was at least one would-be TT from Germany - Ardon Krep. His thread lasted, as I recall, about six months starting in December of 2002. He posted on a German site (www.ratedx.com) that no longer exists.

But he was also another "I knew John Titor" TT.

I agree that in general most of our "TT's" have posted from US IP's and have talked about the USA. But as Rainman pointed out, we've had a lot of UK and Australian TT's plus a few Canadian TT's.

But that tends to make sense on an English language site. It wouldn't make much sense for someone to come onto the forum and post in another language. A few people who don't write English have tried to post by using Google Language Translator.

Rainman knows what I mean when I talk about math iterations. It means that you plug a value into the independent variable, have the formula output a result and then plug that value back into the independent variable. You do it several times. It works great to zero in on a value or define some sort of geometry. But...

Try doing that with Google translator. Plug in an English phrase and have it output the translation in another language. Then have it retranslate it back into English. Do a few iterations and the phrase will no longer have anything to do with the original phrase - and it probably won't make a lick of sense. I read, write ans speak Spanish fluently. I've tried this experiment. ROTFLMAO!!!

Now imagine trying to have a conversation with someone via posts using the translator. Its a mess. Iterations using a not-so-good software language translator is at the very least quite humorous - but not very helpful in deliverring content.

So the short of it is that we should expect TT's here who are from English speaking countries.

PS: I have first hand experience with just how humorous the Google translator can be. Pamela and I both used Google translator to post on the German site. Gooz, the site owner - after falling off his chair laughing at our attempts to post in German, finally told us to post in English.
