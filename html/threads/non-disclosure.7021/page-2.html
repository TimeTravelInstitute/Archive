What you must understand though, is that while the "beans" are infinitely spilled and spread across proverbial time infinity amount of times, the spilling event itself is measured and documented with exactness. Time travel is something rarely comprehended because humanity has yet to comprehend infinity. This will be something accomplished by machines far before humanity advances to higher thought processes.

You mean like the laws we have today against committing murder, or against owning a firearm if you are a convicted felon? It is not enough to posit a law. One must also consider the likelihood of that law being followed by one and all. We see with ALL laws we pass in our society today, it is not just a matter of one or two people disobeying important laws. LARGE numbers of people will disobey a law, especially if they see a benefit to themselves. It does not even have to be a large benefit to themselves to break a law of today's technology. Now consider just how huge a benefit time traveling would be, and then estimate accordingly how many people you think will obey these laws. And in time travel, it only takes a few people not obeying such laws for people throughout time to become aware that time travel is a reality. Darby's point stands, completely notwithstanding any consideration you may give for any man-made (and man-enforced, if possible) laws for time traveling.

Exactly. So then if you put these two facts together:
(1) The tendency of some (quite a few) humans to break laws whenever they see a benefit to themselves.
(2) The fact we do not see ourselves inundated with time travelers right now.

What might this tell us about the possibilities of time travel ever becoming a viable technology? As Darby often puts it: "If time travel ever becomes possible we should already know about it right now...where are all the time travelers?"

I would further submit to you that the reason we do not see time travelers is related to the scientific fact that we are not merely traveling through time in our lives, but we are traveling through space-time. This fact is the sole fact that is missing from ALL romantic stories about time travel. We must account for what Einstein taught us about the reality of the fabric of space-time. That is, you cannot ONLY travel through time. You must also be traveling through space. And further to that, your mass MUST also change as you travel through space-time. The greater your rate of passage through space-time the larger you change in mass must be.

When you consider these facts, I suggest to you that the ONLY phenomenon that humans have reported throughout time that might even remotely align itself with the romantic ideas of time travel are UFOs, in that we assume that UFOs have traveled through space (and thus necessarily also through time) to visit us here at earth. It may be that the "aliens in the UFOs" that humans report are the only real (space)-time travelers we will ever come to know about.
