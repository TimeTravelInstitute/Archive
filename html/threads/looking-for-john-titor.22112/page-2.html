You're catching on, old friend. There doesn't seem to have been much money actually made. But the JTF is a legally organized for-profit business as a Florida LLC. And there's the rub - and a clue.

Larry Haber is the attorney and general manager for the JTF. He's not an ambulance chaser scratching out a marginal existence as an attorney. He's one of the Big Boys in the entertainment/movie making world, i.e. he doesn't come cheap. Where's the money coming from for the past decade? Just handling the phone calls for the business is an expense. I don't know about you but if I call my attorney its $375 an hour.

A business can't (legally) lose money for a decade and a half and remain a business. It eventually gets reclassified as a hobby and loses its tax status as a business. So, is he all in as a general partner, doing a favor to a family member/friend or both? Someone is paying the bills thus there has to be some income.

And it is an LLC. Limited liability. The author can remain anonymous, reap the benefits of profit as a limited partner and let Larry run the business.

Have they made a lot of profit? It doesn't appear that that's the case. They probably waited too long to move the commercial interest forward. The story lost steam in the US in 2004 - The Book was pretty much a flop. They made some inroads in Italy with the RAI John Titor episode but that never took hold either. So it seems a bit more likely that this is being done for a family member or friend rather than a general client. Someone is getting the Family Rate.
