That statement is not science...it is a load of rubbish. It is a mistake made by people with only junior school level knowledge of science..who assume that 'sound' is some sort of universally pervasive property when it is not.

Sound is a rather messy classical physics level ( i.e not the fundamental quantum level ) phenomenon that ONLY exists in the presence of a certain density level of atoms or molecules. Sound is basically a pressure wave...and NOT a property of the particles themselves. Below a certain density level, one will not get sound...because the motion of any particle due to the wave will not be enough to cause it to collide with other particles and thus pass on the wave.

Hence..there is no sound in space. Indeed, unlike gravity and electromagnetic effects from Earth that extend to the Moon and beyond....any sound generated on Earth will never get more than 20 or 30 miles up before the air is too thin to carry the sound any more.
