In a recent discussion about numbers with an 8 year old boy, I witnessed the young lad grappling with the concept of infinity. You see, a friend of his told him that numbers have a begining and an end. He, on the other hand, believed they kept going. He couldn't explain why, so I simply told him, "Because you can always add one." He smiled, and walked away.

I then got to thinking about the universe, its origins, the concept of finite matter. I believe that some scientists theorize that the amount of matter in the universe is finite. But, given the concept that the human brain can always add "one" does that disprove the concept of finite matter? Now granted, a thought doesn't have mass and isn't considered matter, but the fact that there is no end to what the human mind can think of has to mean something. If you come up with a final thought, THE LAST THOUGHT, I will just say, THE LAST THOUGHT plus one.
