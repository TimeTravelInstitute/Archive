According to Freud, who has the most extensive field research on dreams,

the components of dreams are from your daily lives. What you dream at night

is most likely related to unprocessed subconscious materials you detect

through the senses during your waking hours. With the media in all of its forms,

including internet, television, magazines, etc, it's probable you've "seen"

or heard things you didn't register at the time. Each moment about 80%

of your sensory information is "censored" by your consciousness for reasons

that if you had to detect and process all of it at the same time,

it would be pretty insane and difficult to survive. I mean think about all

the stimulus around you at any given moment: there are sounds, smells, textures,

temperatures, flavors, most of the time you are not aware of the flavor

of your own mouth--your mind doesn't send that info to your brain unless

the flavor is foreign or delightful--as in food, or when you are sick--

a more unpleasent sensation. Dreams are made up of the 80% of information

that never gets processed. They can be a good insight into your life

and feelings for this reason. Repitition in dreams--a scenario, or object

can give you clues to issues you might otherwise overlook. For example,

I knew a woman once who kept dreaming she had a red purse that was stolen

and she needed to find it because it had all of her personal information inside.

She had variations of this dream for weeks. She swore she never owned a red purse

like that one and had never seen it. A few months later she found out

her husband was having an affair with a collegue at work. She had met this woman

before through her husband's job, and the next time she saw her, the woman

was carrying a red purse. Because this woman did not work, she was a traditional

housewife, much of her "identity" was tied into her husband's --

and her financial security. The "stolen" purse in her dream could represent

this woman's identity as being stolen by the infidelity. Was her dream

a psychic prediction? Probably not. She had seen her husband around this woman

at functions and probably unconsciously noticed their attraction in the form

of body language and subtle gestures, but because the information was "disturbing"

she did not process it, she repressed it and it came out in her dreams.
