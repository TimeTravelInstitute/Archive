I have a problem with infinity. It's a nice thought, but we simply don't have evidence of true infinity. pi continues on forever but it is still a set number.
Click to expand...
A circle is infinite. Or to be more exotic, a Mobius strip is infinite.

Now before you call me on this, let me explain: The question of infinite/finite, like so many other dualistic things in our universe, always reduces back to the concept of perception and your relative point of view. To a being walking on the Mobius strip, he may not come to the realization that it is infinite but repeating. To a being off of (outside the context of) the strip, it is obvious that the strip is finite in spatial terms. Yet this person can still see the tendency towards the infinite in the structure of the strip itself.

Therefore, I maintain that from our current perception and "internal" context of our universe, it is acceptable to say it is infinite. However, at the same time, but in a different context, our universe can be finite. It is all a matter of context.

My thinking on the matter: the division never takes place. The state vector, from an objective point of view, never collapses.

Rather, all possible 'alternates' have always existed, regardless of our perception of time. What is really meant in this case by "alternate" or "parallel" universe is "possible quantum arrangement of space-time". When you look at the entire span of both space and time, you realize that when you calculate all the possible states that differ from one another by the state of a single quantum of one thing or another, you arrive at a very VERY large number. The bit of elementary school vernacular "infinity times infiny minus one" does an alright job of expressing the utter hugeness of the numerical value involved.

The number of quantum states possible is so large that within it, every possible arrangement of matter, no matter how outlandish, exists somewhere. One of those arrangements of matter is the Earth as it exists now. It does not exist against great odds, as is sometimes said, it exists because it must. For it not to exist would be unthinkable, as it would undermine the first principles of this model.

Human minds have evolved to make this tremendous multiplicity of quantum states comprehensible. From our position within three macroscopic dimensions, we can see only one quantum state at a time. Every single exchange of energy on the quantum level represents a transformation of the universe into a different quantum state. At every instant, the universe actually exists in every possible quantum state, the individual states being laid out next to each other along a fifth dimensional axis which I have dubbed "potential". The mechanisms of our brains limit what we see each second to the quantum state translated to us through the photons that the quantum exchanges create.

So basically, the world is constantly dividing into countless alternate versions of itself. There is no paradox, because the 5 dimensional universe is large enough to contain all possibilities. The division is not truly a division, but our limited perception of what is really a structural branching. It appears as a division because we are trapped moving forward through time at a comparable rate to one another and our surroundings.

The universe is at once much simpler and much more complex than we imagine, or can imagine.

By the way Transient001, don't take this model as a statement of agreed-upon fact. Last I checked, a great many actual physicists discredit the infinite parallel theory. I don't know how true this model is, just that I really, really like it.
