I heard about the John Titor story from a friend of mine who was following it avidly while it was going on. I only stumbled across the archived posts a few days ago, and I have to say my interest was instantly piqued.
Despite the hard-to-swallow nature of the claims Titor made, I am unwilling to form an an opinion to the exclusion of all others. There are just too many unknowns. It is as impossible to prove that he was not a time traveller as it is to prove that he was. All we have is this secondhand information (aside from those who claim to have actually me him and seen the time machine in operation), so declaring faith or disbelief seems kind of silly to me.
I personally see no reason why, based upon his claims, he must NOT have been a time traveller. Sure, he was vague on some points and at times seemed deliberately obtuse, but it is rather remarkable A: how consistent his story remained, B: how genuine and not-forced his language seemed, C: how convincingly nonchalant he seemed in his knowledge of time travel technology and logistics, and D: how intuitively sensible his descriptions of the superverse seemed.
I have spent a good portion of my life pondering the mechanisms of time travel and resulting paradox. After mind-bending consideration of things such as the grandfather paradox and closed temporal loops that normally trip up peoples' thinking on the subject, the conclusions I finally came to about the nature of space-time yielded a universe which is very much like John Titor's. Why? Because it is elegant. It is much more reasonable to allege a universe of infinite parallel worldlines in which no paradox can occur than one which can be stricken with fatal flaws when its inhabitants achieve certain technologies.
My guess is that if time travel is possible, a model of the universe like Titor's would be necessary to allow for it. However, if such a model of the universe is correct, the likelihood of a time traveller arriving in our specific worldline as opposed to one next door a few angstroms is vanishingly small.
So: was John Titor a time traveller? Maybe. Was John Titor convincing in the role of a time traveller? Extremely. I think that a genuine TT, or at least one from as nearby as 2036, would probably act very much like John if he was to reveal his existence in the first place.

The problem with that statement is that if it is accepted as the criteria for truth testing for this or any other scenario then lying is equally as valid as telling the truth. All propositions, no matter how outrageous are equal.

For example: I may be wrong, but I'll assume that you've never been to the top of Mt. Kilimanjaro in Tanzania. You may not know this but the last three hundred feet up the mountain is actually coated with chocolate cream cheese. I challenge you to prove me wrong o to even find a single reference anywhere that says that it is not cream cheese (chocolate or otherwise).

If we are to accept your logic on the Titor issue then my statement is true until proved otherwise.

There is, in fact, an overwhelming body of very solid evidence to support the statement that the Titor Saga is a work of fiction.

Boomer was a very good writer in the most general sense. But when it came to the physics of time travel his base knowledge was almost nil. At best about all one can really conclude is that he he read Steven Hawking's "A Brief History of Time" and Michio Kaku's "Hyperspace" - two pop-sci books on the general subject.

You caught me making the all too common human mistake of overextending what I myself know and applying it to everyone. Rather than saying it is impossible to prove or disprove his story, I should have said that I possess too little evidence in one direction or the other to prove or disprove it to myself.
There is probably enough evidence for someone who really wants to disprove John Titor's story to do so, at least to his or her own satisfaction. But of course, someone else doing this has no bearing on what I know about the story, which is really very little.
There is also the tricky buisiness of all this going primarily over the internet, a medium which keeps real verification quite difficult. As for Kilimanjaro, I could prove to my satisfaction that it is actually snow and not cream cheese by going there and seeing for myself. But since all I have on John Titor is a large amount of text and a few apocryphal photographs, I choose to not even touch the subject of the story's truth. I will do my best in the future to make explicit that I do not expect any opinion I state to extend to anyone else. I merely slipped this time. That is actually a standard which I do my best to hold myself to.
