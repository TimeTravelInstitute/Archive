I took my first lower division college astronomy course at UCSB in 1974. In that class the professor discussed the "problem" with Pluto and the fact that it was not a planet in the sense of the other planets.

It wasn't so much its size but the fact that its orbit was highly eccentric, that it crossed inside the orbit of Neptune and did not track anywhere near the plane of the ecliptic as do the other planets.

Its orbital mechanics indicated that it did not form in the same way that the other eight planets formed.

Since then thousands of "asteroids" have been found in the area of Pluto making Pluto just another huge rock following the same general orbital path.

Astronomers have pondered reclassifying Pluto to something other than a planet for decades.
