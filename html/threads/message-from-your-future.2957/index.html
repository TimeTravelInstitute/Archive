That's the contradiction -- time travelers seem to be bound by a Star Trek-esque "Prime Directive". They can't give any info specific enough to prove that they're from the future, so they hope that people will assume they're from the future.

Logically, any supposed time-traveler that does this is NOT a time-traveler. If you were going to travel back in time and you wanted to let people know about it, you would give proof. It's a situation with two clear choices:

1) You're a time-traveler who has come back. You intend to never let anyone know you're a time-traveler, so you never tell anyone.

2) You're a time-traveler who has come back. You intend to let people know you're from the future -- without any sort of proof, they wouldn't believe you were from the future, so what's the point?

Now, there's a third option, which many of the supposed "time-travelers" follow. It's basically "I'm a time-traveler from the future. My rules allow me to tell you I am, but not show you any proof. So you'll basically have to believe everything I say on pure faith alone. Thanks!"

It's entirely illogical, and obviously not true.

Or you can just do what Titor did -- make some predictions and claims that are a few years off, and then bail out after a couple months. You'll be long gone when it's supposed to happen. (Don't forget to add deniability -- worldlines work well.)
