I'm from the future

I'm from the future. It's true. I've had to work out your unneccesarily confusing email system to tell you.

Anyway, i'm trapped. I wasn't supposed to end up here. I got here following a big fight at a timeport, which, even in the 23rd century, is a luxury way to travel. All i've got is a SuperBANK-o card, which your machines don't accept, and a clever way of getting into cyber cafes free of charge.

My TTranslate it's working well either. It isn't recognised on most of your cybernetic simpletons. I can't even show you my evidence as I don't have a way of storing photos. I do, though, have rather good ways of translating. One of them is to listen to others and grasp on phrases I recognise from my Classic Language lessons at school. Another is to ignore them and watch their facial expressions to try and work out what they're saying.

Anyway. News. Important. I hear of John Titor. He was a celebrity once. Will be, sorry. And was.

Alas, a shock: there's no big galactic empire out there. Only a vast, empty waste of space. There's life, but no galactic politics holding it together. Earth will become the cradle of all civilisation.
