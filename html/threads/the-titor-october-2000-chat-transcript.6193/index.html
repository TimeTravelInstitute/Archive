A bit of a caveat on writing style metrics and analysis. When we were doing that on the posts we had a much larger database for comparison (rthough it was still somewhat small for doing a proper analysis). His posts were written at leisure with time to think about responses, make edits and then post. The IRC was written in real time with no time to think it through. In fact you can see from the transcript that they were all trying to get responses in at the same time and they came out of order. A very different style can emerge under those circumstances. It's more like talking than writing.

It took me from late 2001 until April of 2004 to convince her to register there. She wasn't much interested. But she ultimately did register and is a regular. True...but the fact is that there are certain writing foibles that are almost unconcious, and which tend to exist regardless of the amount of time available. One of them is the habit....I even do it myself....of joining bits of sentences together with multiple dots.

The original Titor chat transcript contains many examples of Titor doing just that. Whereas the anomalies posts contain hardly any such cases in over 100 posts from Titor. Not just a minor difference !

I've read that the use of dots like that is due to those who seek to divide sentences into distinct thoughts.....as opposed to the grammatical use of commas. It's habitual, and when you think about it, doing a bunch of dots takes longer and really ought to be LESS prevalent in chat than in a forum. Yet we have the reverse. Many examples in the chat transcript......barely a single case in the anomalies forum.

Most of those who have that habit can barely go a few sentences without doing it. That is what persuades me that the person in anomalies is not the same Titor as in the chat.
