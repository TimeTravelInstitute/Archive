Re: Reactor's Paranoid Assumptions

Well...

I can't say that I haven't had my moment's with some people now and again during the years of posting here. For me, my sense of humor sometimes is mis-understood, as happened in my spoof thread OvrLrdLegion Travel's in Time Story.

It was taken as serious by some people, and not seen as merely satire. I did reply with some heat in that thread, and maybe a few others, but really didn't take it personally. When I turn off my computer, and walk away, they are all gone. I don't really know "who" they are, and being a "fictional" forum to begin with, I try not to let anyone affect my day.

Not everybody is diplomatic in word structure, and sometimes the true intentions aren't apparent. I try to look past the comments that may be somewhat bothersome, but also try to consider what the author is really trying to convey.

I know that with some of my "wild" theories, you, Rainman, Darby and many others all force me to pause and think about my presentation(s).

I will admit that sometimes there are words written that irk me, being a man with emotions, it is hard to not feel the "bite" sometimes, but have to keep things in context.

Anyone here should never allow sentiments here to result with words being used that can't be taken back, and will forever remain as a testament for anger and hatred.

Sometimes the bickering here makes me all the more effective when I DO get into a debate with people in the "real" world. I feel sorry for those folk's who try to go up against any regular member of TTI in a debate outside these hallowed halls. We all have been specially trained and have experience under our belts that make us formidable opponents.

As it has been pointed out, TTI is still far less critical than some forums, and what is happening here is not all that unique nor different. Try to present a different perspective in most of the scientific communities, and heated replies should be expected. Goes with the territory.

In reading a theory regarding the migration of mankind in one website, and found evidence that was contrary to what the author claimed. I presented that author with the evidence that I discovered, and his reply makes the comments here seem like rainbow's and fluffy marshmellows.

I agree with you that perhaps there are different methods of being critical, without becoming personal in the critical response's. However, we all are different and respond in different ways, and that can't be helped, nor should be desired.

We ALL have an opportunity to learn from our mistakes, and mistakes we ALL will make, and hopefully we ALL learn more about ourselves and apply what we see in ourselves, and elements as pointed out by observers outside of our selves, to become that much of a better person, no matter where we ALL find ourselves.

Life is tough, alot of "stuff" to be dealt with, such as finding out my wife has a disease that may render her blind in the next few years. I really do like everybody here, and find a haven of sorts from the pressure and realities of the real world, and I agree that when these posts become personal battlefields, it takes away from what TTI "should be" all about for everyone.

This is only an opinion, and I learned a long time ago, opinions don't count for much.

Still worth a shot to try to make a better place in the world, today, however unrealistic such an idea might be.
