It's basic quantum mechanics, really.

You *can* change the past in microscopic ways. Indeed, such changes occur spontaneously all the time. But as the objects get larger, affecting change becomes more of a problem.

Physicists of the late 20th have already demonstrated this fact with a calculation involving billiard balls. If a billiard ball goes through a wormhole into the past and hits an earlier version of itself, the collision will cause the ball to fall into the hole - just as it did originally. In other words - sending the ball back in time does not alter the past. This is a general result.
