I'm Jay Cheel, the director of the film mentioned above. The filmu is actually a documentary short, about 24 minutes long. The story of John Titor is only an element of the film and it is pretty much told through Oliver Williams (webmaster of www.johntitor.com) and Larry Haber (Titor family attourney), so the story is pretty intact. Here's a short synopsis of the film:

'Obsessed & Scienfiic' is not wrapped in theories and scienfiic explanations. It looks at people who are sincerely hopeful of a future where time travel is a possibility.

Michio Kaku, the world renowned theoretical physicist, discusses the many problems and possibilities of travelling through time. Rob Niosi, a stop-motion animator and prop builder from New York, discusses his ongoing quest to build a full scale replica of the time machine featured in the 1960 film adaptation of H.G. Well's classic story. Finally, Oliver Williams introduces us to John Titor, a supposed time traveller. Oliver's website, www.johntitor.com, has posed some interesting questions and theories based on predictions and posts left behind by the man from 2036. Entertainment lawyer Larry Haber's involvement in the story goes even deeper, representing the modern day family of the time traveller, acting as a messenger between his clients and the world.

Whether or not time travel exists is not the question at hand here. What's equally intresting is the question of why we are so eager to go back? To relive the best moments of our lives? To change the mistakes we've made?

Obessed & Scientific will be entering film festivals over the next year in hopes of obtaining funding for a feature length version of the film. For more information, visit www.figure26films.com or email jcheel@sympatico.ca.
