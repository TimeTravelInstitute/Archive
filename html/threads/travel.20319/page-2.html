The thing is this (of several). If a real time traveler goes into the past and affects it, has causality been preserved? The cause comes from the future of the event. Or will one argue that the future from which the cause originated was caused by the past?

However, I think a better refutation is that a cause can originate from a level independent of the physical universe, say the spiritual plane. This is not part of the causal history of the physical universe.Or suppose, a visitor from a completely independent realm of existence decides to drop by and see what we are doing. That intrusion is outside of the history of physical causation.
