Javier,

And anyone who doesn't allow them selves to be bullied & shut up by this approach
Click to expand...
Here's the rub. On an online forum it isn't possible to shout anyone down nor is it possible to bully them - unless they allow it to occur.

Only one person at a time can post and only one person at a time can respond. Unlike a live forum where several voices can drown out a single voice, on an Internet forum there are no actual voices. However what tends to occur is that once a written challenge is issued someone (or several people) get their tail feathers in a dither and feel that their "manhood" (or womanhood) is being challenged. They conclude that they are obligated to respond in kind - and so they do.

But what people don't consider is the obvious: that the site, not the individual, is under assault and that the intent is to bring down the site itself - not the individual.

And that's what I want the regulars to consider. Are the initiators of the flame wars intending to discredit the individuals or is it their intent to bring down the site itself?

I'm not going to offer an opinion. I can only say that the best way to make sure that the latter doesn't occur is to consider the source (usually a relatively new member) and to consider responses accordingly.

Yes, I know that there are many regular members who frequently engage in heated debate with other regular members. There's a big difference, however, between that sort of debate and a debate with a new member. The regulars know each other and even though they might engage in heated debate in public they oft times have a back channel communication (email or PM for instance) where they privately draw reasonable lines. You and I, for instance, have done just that more than once. I've done so with many people. Our agreed upon intent has been to offer divergent and well defended opinions on a single subject for the benefit of the audience. That's perfectly legitimate and no one is going to complain. .

I'm not generally attacking new members in saying this. We need new membership. Not all new members have appeared to have come onto the forum to be chaotically disruptive. But I'm asking you and the other regulars to consider that it might be possible that they are being drawn into conflict by some new members who have alternative motives - that their motives might have less to do with the obvious topic at hand than they have to do with being generally disruptive for malicious purposes.

The long and the short of it is to simply ignore the disruption and only respond to new members in a neutral fashion (and save the heat for those members whom you trust to have seasoned and thick skins ).
