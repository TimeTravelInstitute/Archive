Do I \"Believe\" in Titor?

Daniel,

I do believe that Titor was "real" in the limited sense: there is a real human being behind the moniker.

Is he a real time traveler is the appropriate question. Absolutely not.

His travel through time is in the same direction as the arrow of thermodynamics - forward. Time passes for him, and for any other local frame that he observes, in a virtual one-to-one relationship: no measurable time dialation.

The chance the he, or anyone else for that matter, is a time traveler who travels against the arrow of thermodynamics (backwards) is basically zero. Unless and until I see a rigorous theory of physics that proposes backwards time travel I'll remain unconvinced. So far there is no such theory.

The best that can be imagined is based on a few (highly questionable) interpretations of GTR where the time coordinate has to be assumed to be negative based on an "imaginary" square root of "-1"...and where one has to dismiss the inertial mass limitations of "c" or "c+" velocities and which ignores "c" itself as a limiting factor. It basically says that the theory is used to prove backward time travel if, and only if, you accept that the theory is otherwise wrong. Wow...that's a lot to accept.

There are also a few black hole theories involving Kerr Black Holes that propose backward time travel...and which also posit that the wormhole always lies in the future light cone of the would-be time traveler and which further posit that the wormhole is so unstable that it would collapse at the instant that the mass of the time traveler enters it...thus crushing the time traveler into a very fine sub-atomic "dust".

Pretty tenuous on both counts.

Yes, I see a lot of New Age theories that posit God-only-knows-what process allows time travel. Those theories cannot be disproven because they are based on faith. Faith is good but faith is unquestioning belief. There's no use in arguing "facts" with such a theory. There's also no use in trying to prove a faith based theory for the same reason: there's no underlying and repeatable logic to question or explore. One either believes or doesn't believe.
