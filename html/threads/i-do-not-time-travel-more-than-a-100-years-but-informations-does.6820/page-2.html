And the rub.

In an earlier post jcpo speculates that the Giza pyramid is a conductor. Now we jump to the question of, "Why did [ancient Egypt] need electricity?"

But in between we've been presented no evidence that the pyramid is indeed a conductor or that ancient Egypt used electricity. Absent the pyramid at all, if you go up in the air 140 m (about the height of the Giza pyramid) the natural electric potential (voltage) is about 14,000 V. The potential between the air and ground increases at ~100 V/m.

So how did we jump from speculation about the pyramid being a conductor to asking the question why ancient Egypt needed to use electricity? (Or was you question questioning the premise itself?)
