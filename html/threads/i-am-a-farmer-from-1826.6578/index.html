Greetings time travelers,

I am in need of your assistance. I am a farmer from Kentucky of the the year 1826. I have found myself to be in the year 2012. This event, through providence or misfortune, has left me bewildered and miserably in want of my home back in my proper time.

I have no sort of time traveling machine but was brought here through a supernatural experience.

Near where I live, there is a very small cave. Upon entering the cave, I had unwittingly been thrown into some strange vortex. My next memory was exiting the cave to find a much different terrain with buildings made of bright, shiny materials and a larger crowd than I have seen before. Since that time, I have been staying in a homeless shelter as I have no current residence.

As I write this message, I am seated in a library in front of this computer. The library assistant is very helpful.

Pardon my candor, but this experience has been frightful for me and I pray that a time traveler can help me get home soon.

Greetings my friends,

I have been busy adjusting myself to my temporary stay in Adam's home. I should mention his and his family's benevolence in taking me in and the warm treatment I am receiving.

Skarpz, I am afraid Oregon would be far too great a distance to imagine traveling. I will be certain, as I said before, to notify my wife of your relatives and have her send them a letter. Of course, we shall relate my story to them. Whether they would believe it or not is another matter.

Concerning their burned house you mentioned, should there be a message you would have me pass on to them?

Transient001, Before I respond to your question about the seed planter, let me make one important point as delicately as I can.

A farmer knows his tools and equipment. If, for example, a farmer should plant a crop such as tobacco, he knows the tools and equipment he needs for the tillage of that crop. Generally, a farmer does not know the tools he does not use.

My answer to your question is that I do not know of this seed planter's weakness. I do not use such a thing.

My crops include wheat and oat. I use a single-furrow plough drawn by my one horse. The seeds are scattered once the soil is tilled properly.
