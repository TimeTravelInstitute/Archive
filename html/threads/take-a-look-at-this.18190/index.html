There are a few issues to take into consideration here. As of now, our technology is far too primitive for a Matrix-like contingency to arise. Let's look at what would need to occur in order for the nightmare to come true.

First: We would have to create machines that can design other machines of equal to or greater than their own complexity. We already design and build machines with machines, but currently the level of complexity decreases drastically from generation to generation. For example, a gigantic factory full of specialized robots, with instructions from computers, puts together a car. The Matrix would require factory robots that could build soldier robots, or reconnaisence robots, etc.

Second: The human element would need to be removed from the design and construction process. Right now, those factories building cars require humans to stand around and push buttons, troubleshoot, and generally do all the thinking. The robots of today are just tools. In order for the Matrix to come about, the machines would have to be MINDS.

Third: The machines would have to want to take over. Fearing today's robots ruling us is as silly as fearing today's cuisinarts and sports cars ruling us. They don't want to, because they can't want. So what would it take for machine intelligence to equal human intelligence? It would probably require a highly parameterized and naturally evolved quantum computer. That is essentially what the human brain is, after all. All the computers of today are basically atomic in nature. Little switches, either on or off. We're working on quantum computers, which theoretically could rival the human brain for adaptability and cunning.

I would say it might be dangerous to set up an experiment in which quantum computers are left alone to design better quantum computers. It would especially be dangerous to then let those evolved computers have free reign over versatile manufacturing facilities. But really, when it comes down to it, humans would have to hand over the world to machines on a silver platter in order for the Matrix to come about. Even the most advanced machines only do what we tell them to do. They have not evolved to fight for survival or dominance as we have, and there is no reason for them to do so.
