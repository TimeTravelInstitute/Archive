ALTERING THE COURSE OF HISTORY
Find out why theoretical physicists think changing the past is impossible.
Travelling back in time now becomes very confusing when you are twenty.
The moment you are in the past you seem to have control over what you allow and don't allow to happen. You might be able to approach your younger self and tell him all the mistakes you will or have made.

In an accident you might give your teenage self bad advise. You might cause a chain reaction of events that make youself get badly bullied. Suppose you advise yourself to just ignore it instead of asking for help and HAVING it stopped. You look back on your childhood as if you know and remember everything about it. But of course if you are making changes this is no longer the same as your youth. The timeline has split and you are going up a different root in time.

The point is you don't know what like for your younger self. The pain! Other pupils mocking him every day. This could lead him to his grave. At sixteen unable to take it any longer you might commit sucide. This means you are dead before you are able to go back in time! You don't get the chance to go back in time. You never go back and meet yourself and never give any advice good or bad. You do not affect history.

You cannot change the past. In the sci-fi series Crime Traveller this idea was used. Holly (the quantum physicist who had a time machine)was never able to interfere with events that have happened. It was the first law of time. In simple terms you can visit history but you can't change it.

Maybe go INTO different universes. After going back in time the first change you make might put you in a different world. You can kill you grandmother when she was a girl. At her funeral you are still alive. How since you have never been born? You have came FROM a parallel alternate universe where you actually have been born. When you kill your grandmother you are actually killing a carbon copy her double FROM this world. Your double FROM this world will now never be born because of your actions.

IMPOSSIBLE
The following case of events could not happen.
Since the present is after the past do research now about a trip you will make back in time briefly. In a drawer you find a contract saying "I have succeeded in time travel to the past"signed by you. You cannot remember signing this before because you haven't yet. The wall clock reads 10:08Using a wormhole you go back five minutes ago. The clock now reads 10:03You pull the drawer open and grab the contract. It is not signed. You hear the handle of the door being turned. Your other self comes INTO the room. After burning the contract noone is going to find it. Accidently the place caughtes fire. Both of you run out. You other self cannot understand what's going on. After tripping down stairs he can't get up. Fire is all around him but the major threat is the smoke. After breathing in too much smoke death follows.


Follow a simpler example. If the Doctor using his TARDIS arrives in earth just before 2000 again and shot his other self there. This is an experiment to see what would happen. After the doctor shots his otherself in the head, the wounded doctor dies. What happens to the doctor that came back? He's already dead before he had a chance to come back. Would he therefore fade out of existance when he kills his other self? That would go against the laws of physics for this world.

The doctor could not have killed his other self. It is a paradox which is impossible.

Dear Paul:

If everything requires a cause, then how did the universe get started in the first place? Clearly, even if cause-and-effect relationships are a given in this world, right now, at some point in the distant, distant past, this was not the case. (Or somehow the future created the past, which is one of the famous "paradoxes.") I tend to think that time is largely a phenomenon of perception, but not necessarily a part of "objective reality" (whatever that means. WE MUST RE-THINK CLASSICAL PHYSICS AND CAUSALITY. Many people need a belief in cause-and-effect relationships in ORDER to continue operating effectively in the world. But what if cause-and-effect is not absolute at all, but only a tendency? After all, there are so many causes and so many effects, all interwoven, and my experience tells me that free will does indeed exist to some limited extent.

Best Wishes on your OBE attempts!
