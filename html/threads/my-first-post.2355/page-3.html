Re: The Chronoanthropologist

Perhaps, Chrono, your major mistake in this issue of the "Lizarans" speaking English is one of mere probability.

Granted, an infinite universe such as ours is suspected to be will contain an infinite number of intelligent species. An infinite number of those species will speak English. An infinite number of them will speak a variant of English that differs only by the omission of the word "lungfish".

In an infinite universe, all possibilities will come to pass somewhere. Perhaps this crossed your twelve-year-old mind and struck you as the mind-boggling notion that it is. But Chrono, the issue with this, just like the issue of whether aliens are visiting Earth today, is not whether or not they exist; it is whether or not they have had time to reach us. The answer to that for the alien question is maybe, depending on various factors. The answer to the infinite English speaking species question is No. They will never reach us. You would have to travel an unthinkable distance, far greater than the diameter of the observable universe, to get there.

Perhaps this is not your rationalization for why the Lizarans speak English. Maybe you gave the reason in elsewhere. I may be mistaken. But if this is an issue of pure probability, keep this in mind: the chance of another intelligent species within travelling distance of Earth just happening to develop a language identical to English is, for all intents and purposes, impossible.
