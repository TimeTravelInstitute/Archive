For the record, this is false. I believe what we have here is a case of absence-of-evidence being mistaken for evidence-of-absence, a common mistake among humans.

Dr. Sagan certainly believed that there is life beyond Earth, or more accurately, did not even see it as a matter of belief. Mere statistics predict it as more than likely that the universe is teeming with life. What Sagan did NOT believe is that any of this life HAS VISITED EARTH, at least within the lifespan of human history. It's no trick for life to evolve out of pre-biotic muck. It is quite a trick for this life to survive long enough to evolve into intelligence, for that intelligence to create civilization, and for that civilization to last long enough without obliterating itself to venture out among the stars and make contact. These are totally different issues: the laws of nature versus the luck and cleverness of sentient minds.

To paraphrase Sagan in his own novel, Contact: if we are alone in the universe, doesn't it seem like a pretty big waste of space?
