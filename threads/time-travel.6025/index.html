There always is the possibility that one might begin with good intention's, and when the ability is achieved, the intention changes.

I like to think that IF I was able to travel through time, it would be my intention to witness historical events, save historical manuscripts and texts ( i.e., before the library at Alexandria got all burnt up ), and answer some personal questions regarding certain mysteries.

However, this is what I am thinking without actually being able to travel through time.

IF I discovered that I actually could travel through time, there would certainly be some desire of ill-intent that would come to mind, as well. I would like to believe that I wouldn't want to go back through time and whomp a childhood bully. However, it would be extremely hard to resist the temptation to give the bully a surprise whomping that I feel he deserves.

OR

IF an event occurs that hit's a nerve, such as a child abduction and crime, would the temptation to travel back in time and try to prevent the crime become too much ? And if one begins to administer justice, where does one draw the line with what would be appropriate AS justice ?
