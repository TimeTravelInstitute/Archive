Morgan, Stanford White and other backers withdrew support Tesla because Tesla seriously underestimated the cost. The tower was huge and expensive but, as it turned out (as Tesla admitted in a 1901 letter to White) too small to accomplish the intended task. It needed to be three times larger (over 1000 ft tall).

If you read Tesla's papers on the project they are punctuated many, many times with "if we assume" and "suppose" as he stated his design. That's fine for theoretical electrodynamics but is not so good for applied electrical engineering. It was not ready for Prime Time.
