This is my first post here. I did a lot of thinking about traveling back in time for years as I feel I was born in the wrong time. I would like to share some thoughts and ideas I discovered along the way.I have separated each thought as it's own paragraph.

1. I am of the firm belief you cannot change time. That let's say you go back to 1956 you were always there in the first place. There would be records of your 1956 existence. This also explains away paradoxes and I feel is the most logical explanation. The movie Somewhere In Time uses this model of time travel and explains it perfectly.

2.Most people wish to time travel to right a wrong or to redo or fix an aspect of their life.
Once you realize you cannot change anything there really is only 2 reasons to time travel.
To visit a different time to see how things were and learn from the experience. Like going to Colonial Williamsburg but for real. The other reason being you feel like I do and wish to start a new life in another time.

3.Issues with the last thought. Traveling to another time is not like traveling to another state or country. A lot of things can happen. Let's say you get stuck in that time and cannot return to the present. Are you prepared to live out your life in another time? Most people would not.

4.Let's say you are willing to take the risk of getting stuck or are willing to live out the rest of your life in another time. Would you be able to make all the sacrifices need to do so? Let's say you go back to 1956. You would not have any of the technology or really much of anything you use in day to day life. No cell phones, no computer, no cable, no color TV, no credit cards, no ATM, no disposable razors, e-mail, heck in a lot of places no a/c. Would you be able to give up all of that?

5.Then there is the fact you would have no family or friends. Most likely you will find your friends or family will not make the trip with you because of the loss of friends and family or idea #4 above. Would you be ok with being in a new world foreign to you all alone with no friends or family. Would you be willing to leave all of them behind and start a new?

6.You are ok with the above and decide to go back to 1956 alone. You also have no identity. How would you obtain legal documents like a driver's license, birth certificate, passport? Are you willing to find a legal way to do all of this in that time or take the risks involved with doing it illegally? What would you do for work? What if your current list of job skills are unneeded in 1956? There would be no need for IT professionals, graphic artists, programmers or any other host of modern jobs. You could get a job as a car mechanic, or even a computer programmer back in 1956, but you would have to learn job skills of the time with what was then current technology it programming with punch cards or rebuilding a carburetor.

7.Why does the government not have time travel and why do we not see time travelers from the future in day to day life? Well, if you agree with any of the above or think about it since you can;t change time there really isn't much of a reason for doing so. Not many people would be willing to live in a past time and give up all the would have to. And if they were, they would blend right in and there would be some kind of historical records of their existence thus no paradoxes. Day trippers could go to another time without detection. Day trippers would go to places like NYC frequented by other tourists and would go about unnoticed and since they were always there in the first place, their being he does not effect anything.

I am sorry for such a long first post, but I hope it will at least get everyone to think and to realize all the secondary efforts one would have to make even after they find the way to travel in time.

Correct. If you find a way to TT into the past the logic says that whatever occured, according to the history you were aware of before you traveled to the past, is the correct history that included your presence and actions.

At least that's the macro-world Newtonian logic. There is another way of looking at it that doesn't call upon the Many Worlds Interpretation. In a post earlier during the week I referenced the "indistinguishibility of particles" principle of QM. One proton (neutron, etc.) is just like every other proton. The same is true of like molecules. Two molecules equally constructed are virtually exact copies of each other. There's no way to tell one from another.

Now we can look at the Mansfield example from the macro-world view and see drivers, passengers, cars and the scenery, distinguish which is which and trace their histories leading up to and including the event (traffic accident and death). However, if we get really close up to the event all we can see is a collection of organic and inorganic molecules, chains of protein, gasses and what-not, that don't appear to be well organized. In fact, at that level we see mostly a vacuum with atoms spaced very far apart even it the atoms bound up in molecules. They are giggling about in what appear to be random motions. Because one like molecule is a copy of every other like molecule it makes no difference at all, from the physics POV, whether the molecule appeared from the present, past or future. At that level there is no paradox. A molecule from the future isn't going to ask how it got there or what "odd" effect it might have on another molecule. It's going to interact with the surrounding atoms in accordance with the rules of quantum electrodynamics (QED)...what we usually call chemistry. "Changing the past" at least at the quantum level is not paradoxical or precluded on first principles. Based on first principles at the quantum level it might not even make sense to consider whether or not the past can or can't be changed. There are, of course, other physical effects going on - most of which involve symmetry and conservation laws - that have to be considered as we test the theory.

So, as we ponder the implications of time travel, we should keep in mind the laws of physics and remember that especially in quantum physics that what we view as common sense and logical from the Newtonian world is a bias on our part that in many cases doesn't reflect how the world actually works.

Why don't we see time travelers (real ones - not the Internet Fruitbat variety)? All theories of time travel to the past are based on General Relativity, Einstein's theory of gravity. Accomplishing time travel to the past involves warping spacetime into a loop that leads to a spacetime coordinate where the time coordinate is earlier than "now". In other words the gadget has to have a roadway leading to the past. This means that the roadway had to exist in the past and the gadget had to be on that roadway in the past. The roadway is created the instant that the gadget is activated and extends toward the future so long as the gadget remains in the "on" position. If it's a man made gadget then time travel to the past is limited to the instant that it is turned on. If we have no such device today then time travel from the future to today is precluded. If the gadget is a celestial object like a black hole that was formed from a super nova a billion years ago then the roadway extends back one billion years.
