Evidence of your limited knowledge of systems.

To your first statement: Chaotic behavior CAN be found in any system that has closed-loop feedback (either natural or human-induced). Furthermore, ALL systems are impacted by some form of feedback. Finding chaos in any system is merely a matter of what state variables you wish to observe over time.

To your second statement: This is laughable, outdated thinking. Outdated because in the past scientists and engineers used to ignore "higher order effects" (i.e. ones which corresponded to 3rd, 4th, or higher order derivatives of states). We have since updated our knowledge that these higher order effects (which can be and are related to interactions we did not consider) can become quite dominant in the time and/or frequency response of a system. In fact, being a control systems engineer by trade, I am now working in areas where we USED to try to linearize all our control laws but now have learned that non-linear controls (which take into account higher order effects) can be MUCH more energy efficient.

So to correct your last statement: All systems do not necessarily have high gain paths that interact with other systems. However, all systems do, indeed, interact with other systems, if even at low gain levels or higher-order derivative effects.

We live in one, massive, closed-loop universe.

First, let me say welcome to TTI, blu64.

You have asked some very intriguing questions, and I see that your train of thought has led you into a philosophical territory very similar to the one I've been wandering around in for years. The basic gist of what you're wondering about seems to be the degree to which consciousness and force of will can sculpt our subjective realities. It seems to me this is one of the core quandries of the human experience: how much power do we and our minds have, and how far does free will really go?

I happen to believe that conscious will is probably our most powerful tool. I think it is our consciousness that carves the meaningful patterns of our lives out of a sort of undifferentiated sea of potentials. Most of us go through life believing that we can basically only affect changes to the world by direct applications of our actions or words. My suspicion is that these things are in truth secondary. Our primary creative act occurs before we ever put our hands to tools and materials. Most of this creative act occurs subconsciously, in systems of neurology evolved over millions of years. But I also believe it to be true that through self-discipline and lucidity of thought a person should be able to, at least in a limited capacity, will reality in various ways.

For example, I have devoted a good part of my effort for the past several years to noticing and contemplating instances of synchronicity, or meaningful coincidence, in my day to day life. I have found that the more I notice them, the more connections I draw between apparently disparate things, the more similar connections I can "create". I believe that if this discipline is take far enough, there would be no reason one could not will their subjective reality into a totally different form. Shifting into a past time should be no more difficult than changing some detail about the structure of the world such as who knows who, or what happened when. Such things shouldn't be subject to censorship by paradox either, because there is no literal traveling 'back' in time involved. 'Back' ends up being an invalid concept, and 'time' a useful mechanism or artifact of human neurology.

Of course, there are side effects. As I have travelled farther and farther down this strange path, my way of thinking has diverged farther and farther from that of mainstream humanity.
