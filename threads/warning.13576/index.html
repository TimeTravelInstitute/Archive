Now it is time to turn the "I need a visual" thing back around on you! I need a better explanation of what you mean by "angled more towards the cockpit". Angled in what way?


In general, the sweep angle of the leading edge of an aircraft is dictated by how fast you want it to fly. If you want it to fly supersonic (like the F-23 was designed for), then this is what causes the very large sweep angles. It relates to the strength of the shock wave. Sweeping the wings reduces the component of air velocity that is approaching the leading edge of the wing straight-on. For high subsonic airplanes (like commercial aircraft), the wing sweep is much lower than supersonic...usually around 25 degrees.


It's all about maximizing the aerodynamic efficiency and minimizing drag. And then there are forward swept wings... they result in an inherently unstable airplane. Research the X-29 and you will see a forward-swept wing that actually flew!
