I have experienced this time dilation state, twice in my lifetime. Both times in my early twenties. I was fear motivated. Quite possibly my brain interpreted both situations as imminent death situations. I always thought it was a built in self preservation feature of the brain. In both situations, because everything was slowed down to a crawl speed, I did use the extra mental time to think my way out of these situations. Obviously with success.

The first time it happened, I was driving on the freeway. It was raining cats and dogs outside, and I was traveling at 70 mph. The car lost traction with the road, and began to go into a spin. My entire life flashed before my eyes. But the slowed down time allowed me to think and remember what my driving instructor had taught me about how to get out of a situation like this. His words were, "Turn into the spin until you regain control of the vehicle." Instinctively I didn't want to do that, but I did it anyway. It worked. Shaking like a leaf, I pulled off the freeway onto the shoulder. All the other drivers saw what was happening and slowed to a stop to let me off the roadway.

The second time I was stopped for a red light at a crosswalk waiting for two children to cross the road. A car behind me was approaching too fast to stop. I could see a crash was imminent. Time slowed down again. I had the time to think of a way, to get out of harms way. I tried to move my foot off the brake over to the gas pedal. I remember my leg would hardly respond. It was like my leg only wanted to move in slow motion too. But I succeeded and got my foot onto the gas pedal. Pedal to the metal. The rear tires were squealing, and the car seemed to just sit there. Then the impact occurred. Luckily the two children seen what was happening and didn't cross the road.
