The "tornado" that hits his house is not a tornado, but one end of a wormhole. The plane with his family on it flies near the other end of the wormhole, and the jet engine is deposited back in time just above his house. If he had not avoided being killed by the jet engine the 'first' time around ('first' used loosely, as the instances are not ordinal in sequence), then all the subsequent events of the movie, leading up to his family getting on the plane, would not have happened. Recall the scene in which Donnie talks to his science teacher about time travel, and the teacher tells him about Einstein-Rosen Bridges, also known as wormholes.

The core idea behind the time dysfunction in the movie is "the tangent universe", a concept which I have never heard of outside the context of this film. Do not mistake it for hard physics, it's only a plot device. The idea is that time is circular, and when the paradox is created by Donnie not being killed when he's supposed to, the universe branches and a tangent is created, diverging ever farther from the circle of time. Everyone relating to the events that created the tangent universe becomes trapped in it, and eventually the distortion created by this sequence of events causes the universe and the tangent universe to collapse. By setting his actions right and being killed when he's supposed to, Donnie preserves the proper order of the universe and prevents its destruction.

I think that Donnie's laughing at the end because he realizes his life is a cosmic joke, and that the universe can only continue existing at the expense of his life. He gets kind of a raw deal when you think about it.
