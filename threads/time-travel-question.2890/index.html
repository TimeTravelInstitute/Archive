Saturday, July 28, 1973. Two miles east of here there were over half a million people gathered for Summer Jam. There was a lot of freaky stuff going on, all the roads were blocked with abandoned cars and everyone was on foot, AND a hard rain fell at times. It would be the perfect cover.

I wouldn't come home here, though. I wouldn't choose to meet my younger self and I wouldn't want to put my family through any kind of weird feelings....or fear, for that matter, because who knows how they would react? No, I would head in the opposite direction. Hitch my way to the west coast, telling fantastic stories along the way....the plots of movies that had not been made yet. Nothing about future reality. Nothing that anyone could use to gain an unfair advantage.

From California, I'd head north to Vancouver, British Columbia. I would walk into the Dairy Queen where Dorothy Hoogstraten was working....years before Paul Snider ever came on the scene. I would convince her to leave there. Snider would never even meet her. That sad chain of events would never be.

I would live a simple, unobtrusive life. I would not use my knowledge to gain financial advantage, but neither would I try to play God with the world and attempt to prevent every bad thing that happened. There would be no way to avert every accident, every murder, every tragedy. No one can be Atlas and it would be foolish to try. If I could save just one life that would otherwise be tragically lost, I would feel that I had done something extremely worthwhile. And if Dorothy and I didn't become romantically involved, it would be enough for me just to know that I had been able to save her.
