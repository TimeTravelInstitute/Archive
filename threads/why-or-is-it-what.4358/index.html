Olly,

But truly, It may be that we have little to learn from the time traveler
Click to expand...
That's an excellent point especially given the standard scenario posed by our would-be time travelers. They always point out that they come from some "parallel" nearly the same, but still different, Earth twin.

Other than giving us some specifics on how s/he accomplished the task, i.e. the science behind the gadget, which would be highly instructive, their "history lessons" are generally irrelevent. What happens on their world is unrelated to what will happen on this world. We might learn some general history lessons from his/her story but we have sufficient history lessons from our world to be able to draw our own inferences. Within the context of how they have all phrased the scenarios underlying their stories (Many Worlds, alternate universes) they know nothing more about our future than we know about our future and they have no more ability to discover that future beforehand than we have. According to their stories all that they would be discovering is the history of yet another alternate universe - not ours. The time travel aspect of the story isn't necessary to accomplish their task of giving us doom and gloom political lessons.

But the science behind time travel is their downfall. It was even Titor's downfall. He knew how to Google and find the references. He'd read some pop-sci books on the subject written by physicists. Bur he didn't understand what those references were stating.

For instance he refered us to look at a typical Penrose diagram to see how travel via a black hole's wormhole leads to alternate universes.

We remember that his task was to go to an alternate universe that was very similar to his so that he could obtain the IMB 5100/5110. And we remember his "divergence" theory.

But here's the problem. A Penrose diagram does, in fact, show pathways to alternate universes. But they also indicate pathways to the same universe and allow travel to different places and times within that universe. Its unnecessary to resort to going to another universe if what you want is to obtain some object or information from within your universe. That's part of what I've termed Titor's "silly science".

Caveat: this does not mean that we can actually travel through a wormhole at all. Our current state of the science indicates that this is a theoretical solution that only works in a sterile universe that contains just the black hole and no other matter or energy. In short, the theory only allows black hole time travel in an idealized laboratory setting.

We might, someday, find complete solutions that apply to a real universe that has stars, planets, dust, photons and black holes that formed after the Big Bang.
