BobbyJoe,

Unfortunately the only way that we know of, at least in theory, to time travel to the past is by warping spacetime into a loop where moving forward in time (from the perspective of the time traveler as s/he looks at his clock ticking away inside his "time machine") is along the loop that curves into the past. The only object that we know of that could possibly allow something as large as a person to make such a trip, rather than a single subatomic particle, is a class of multi-stellar mass Kerr-Newman black holes that are both charged and spinning. The problem is that the loop only leads back to the instant that the loop formed. If you form it today and wait ten years you can travel back only as far as today. No more.

So unless someone harnessed a K-N black hole in NC cr. 1860 then 1860 is out. Frankly, Internet time travelers notwithstanding, we're hundreds if not thousands of years away from having the ability to create such an object. Even if we knew the current location of such a black hole and believed that we have the technology to harness it as a time machine we're still at a loss. There's no such black hole within dozens of light years of Earth. Travel slowly and it takes hundreds of thousands of years to make the trip. Travel really, really fast (as in close to the speed of light) the time inside the ship to make the trip is short (several years) but because of the relativistic effect of fast travel the universe still ages thousands of years outside the ship as we make the trip. Damned if you do; damned if you don't.

We certainly can't fabricate such a black hole with the materials we have on Earth. If you used the entire planet to make a black hole it would have an event horizon about 9mm in diameter. Ignoring all the other problems, you'd have to be stretched into a string of ultra-thin spaghetti to thread the eye of the wormhole (actually that's exactly what would happen). The object is to arrive alive in the past not just have your individual atoms arrive in the past.
