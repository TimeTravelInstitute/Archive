There's a big difference in civil law between what can be said in public about "public figures" such as actors, professional athletes, political candidates running for office or elected public officials and what can be said of a private person.

Sure, there are limits in the former case but they are very widely drawn. The common factor between people in those categories is that they make their living on publicity. In the later case of privite individuals there are well defined limits as to what is considered to be protected free speech. Private individuals have a reasonable expectation that private, even controversial, information about their lives won't be posted on a whim in public for several billion people to read. Even newspapers get sued successfully for crossing that line.
