Caesar (accepting the truth of what is undoubtedly a folk tale as opposed to what happened) most certainly did not share my views.

I am a big fan of the many histories theory, and it is great for storytelling purposes to deliberately get it wrong and dive into the whole Sliders type subgenre, but in the here and now- what real difference does it make? Let's all put to one side the hypothetical butterfly effect as well. Butterflies beating wings causing hurricanes is a beautiful piece of poetry but Earth is not a closed system- everything from organophosphates to poison ice comes hurtling down through the atmosphere from space so the butterfly is one of an ever-expanding number of variables.

Also the aetiology of history isn't as simple as the number of steps on a bus. It's somewhat analogous to vectors- the sum of all the historical forces acting produces the result, and the result produced is on average the most likely result to occur. ON AVERAGE. Sometimes less likely results are the ones that happen. But with some sort of predictability.

Assassinations illustrate this well. Public figures who have been successfully assassinated almost always have the odds stacked against them- they rarely have one enemy, they have usually provoked many groups. These groups may hate each other, but they hate the public figure more. Thus many of the co-dependent effects of social cohesion are absent- if enough cops, military, war industry, journalists, organised crime scum and spooks are angry at the same person, they won't be interfering with each other the way they usually do. The oversight disappears. Those who might complain are scared. Those who wish to act feel free to.

The piratical attitudes of many pre-Nazi politicians in Germany; the angst of the population; the appalling economic conditions- that compost was going to spawn SOMEONE.

The upheavals of the 1960s began in the paranoia and aggression of the 1950s; the racial tensions in the US had to resolve one of three ways- liberation, new slavery or civil war; a powerful secret intelligence apparatus; an insider becoming a turncoat after a sudden attack of ethics infuriated the beltway- that kind of brooding dissent was going to cause a wave of assassinations.

Going back to the butterfly, deforestation exascerbates the severity of hurricanes more than butterfly wings cause hurricanes. In a reference frame such as the one where we live the mundane explanations and mundane causes are overwhelmingly more influential than the exotic- on average.
