I went ahead and did some "research" on the subject shortly after the "White Noise" movie hit the theatres. I have never experimented with EVP recordings and never will. I can't say with any certainty what I believe the cause of these electronic recordings are. Are they voices of ghosts? The science in me says likely they are not. I'm a skeptic by nature, especially when dealing with the range of paranormal phenomena. Since I have never experimented with EVP, and won't, because I don't dabble in the art of pseudoscience, and I'm not interested in proving the exsistence of "ghosts" as most of the researchers in EVP are. SO, I did listen to some audio samples from an EVP - related website, can't remember the URL, but the range of distortion and volume of these recordings is so variable, there's really no way to study them objectively. What I mean is, some people may here a voice say a certain thing, some people something entirely else, some people may not here a thing, and if your like me, it sounds like a bunch of electrical interference, or static. This is one of those areas of mental science I've referred to - What do you THINK you heard? What was your emotional state, were you paranoid, frightened?
