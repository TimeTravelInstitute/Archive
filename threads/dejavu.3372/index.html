Hello, i'm new to posting on these forums, but i have been reading these forums for quite a while. I am 16 years old, but please don't judge me by my age, because i'm not your typical teenager.

For possibly years, i've been experiencing what i think is dejavu. To be more specific, sometimes (and it's very random) i dream about a certain situation in my point of view. However, these "visions" only last a few minutes, and they aren't very clear. These situations always involve me talking to someone, or me listening to someone talk. And here's the wierd part. I don't even feel it coming, but all of a sudden, these situations i dreamt about happen in real life, and i can sense that. When a situation i dream about like this really happens, i know it has, because in my mind i'm thinking "wait a minute, i've heard this before" or "this has happened before".

for example, this one day i was in school, and there was a ceremony in our school gym. one of my friends was sitting on the stand right behind me, to my right. Then he wanted to tell me something, and we started a conversation about something. That's when it hit me, i knew at that point that i visioned that happening. The same setting, time, place, and person i was talking to. And what frustrates me is that every time i have one of these visions, it's never significant in my dreams. I always brush it off like it was just a thought or something. Then eventually, it happens, and i remember that i have visioned it happening.

Hopefully everyone has understood what i was trying to say. Feel free to ask any questions.

- Kevin

Whatb you are describing is Deja vu, 'already seen' in french, I couldn't have described deja vu better. We have or I have been trying to tell people that this is just the immense power of the human brain at work in a dreaming state. Look at the thread physic premonitions and future sight, you'll see what I mean.

It doesn't have to be the exact same situation but if its close enough to something your brain has seen or visualised then you will get that feeling of deja vu. It's like most memories, they can be pulled into the conscious state, by the recognition of a few events. But it takes a certain amount of similarity for deja vu to occur. I think; ror example, you might do the same daily routine everyday, the same things, yet never get a feeling of deja vu, you could also dream about this daily routine, and yet never get deja vu, it has to be a set of events beyond the normal occurence.
