I think it's highly unlikely that any time travel laws would ever be enforceable. By the very definition of all the 'timeline' nonsense....going back and stopping the time traveller from doing whatever he was doing would simply generate a new timeline, and not interfere at all.

But also, there would be an ethical problem with any such intervention. The mere existence of a time traveller would change time in due course such that new people would be born who would not otherwise be. To intervene would be to negate the existence of millions of people.

A bit of a scary thought.....you could suddenly cease to exist one day, because the Temporal Police took action against some time traveller.
