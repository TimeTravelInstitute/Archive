What you're really describing, here, is a crustal slip as described by Charles Hapgood. In such a shift, the crust of the earth slips like a loose skin over the mantle. 98 to 99 % of the mass of the earth is in the mantle and core. To make such a shift possible, the transition layer between the mantle and the lithosphere, or crust, liquifies as a result of increased temperature and pressure. Recent earthquakes may indicate that such pressure and temperature is occurring If the crust is unbalanced rotationally, there may be enough torque to cause slippage.A lithosphere imbalance may have existed for some time which was counter-balanced by the weight of ice at the poles. Melting polar ice might expose this imbalance.

This could be quite dangerous to creatures on the surface of the planet at the time.

Anecdotally, in the early 1960's during a channeling session, I asked the channel if such a "pole shift" was in our future. I was told, "Yes, but not in your lifetime."

If you look at some of the predictions made by Cayce carefully, you might note that he had a habit of giving the last two years of the date and not the century. For example, he would say things like "expect it in '58 or '60," (that's just an illustration not a real date), so some of his predictions still have a chance of coming true, should it be 2060 rather than 1960.
